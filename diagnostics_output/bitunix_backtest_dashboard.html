Key Findings

  • Bitunix systematically prices 0.08-0.20% below Hyperliquid across mid-tier coins. This is a persistent bias, not random noise.
  • Strategy C (bidirectional, z=±2.0) is the best risk-adjusted: Sharpe 11.39, max DD only -1.11%. Trades both long and short BX.
  • Strategy B (hold-the-bias) has highest raw returns (+28-72% daily) but larger drawdowns (-16% max DD). Best for aggressive capital deployment.
  • Round-trip costs must stay below 6 bps for the edge to be viable at z=2.0. Use limit orders on both venues.
  • Best coins: NEAR, IMX, ICP, AAVE. LINK is worst — its divergence doesn't mean-revert cleanly.
  • Short BX (when expensive) performs slightly better than long BX (when cheap) — expensive BX is more of an anomaly given the systematic cheap bias.
  • Mean reversion half-life: ~2-3 minutes for extreme dislocations (z > 2.0). Fast execution is critical.
  • At lower thresholds (z=1.0-1.5), transaction costs eat the edge. Only trade at z=±2.0 or higher.

Cost Analysis Takeaways

  • At z=±1.0-1.5, the strategy is unprofitable at any reasonable cost level (even 2 bps RT loses money).
  • At z=±2.0, the strategy is barely unprofitable at 2-4 bps RT but needs sub-2 bps to be clearly positive.
  • Target: sub-6 bps round-trip cost. This means ~2 bps fees + 2 bps slippage per leg using limit orders.
  • Bitunix taker fee: ~0.02%. HL taker fee: ~0.04%. Using limit orders on both venues can reduce this to ~0.01% each.
  • Slippage on BX for mid-tier coins: ~0.01-0.03% for small orders (<$10K). Scale accordingly.
